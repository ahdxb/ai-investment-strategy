# AI-Driven SME Acquisition and Rollup Strategy

*Version: 0.1*

-----

## Investment Thesis (Summary)

Acquire small businesses (<$10M revenue) in fragmented sectors and deploy AI solutions to materially improve profitability, accelerate revenue growth, and enhance scalability. Returns are realized through sustained cash flow generation or opportunistic exit — the strategy is hold-period agnostic, focused on durable value creation.

The competitive edge is not that AI deployment is unique — it will eventually become widespread — but that this investor group can execute it **now**, with superior technology fluency, operational discipline, and a repeatable playbook that compounds across a portfolio of similar businesses.

|Category             |Examples                                                  |SME Maturity|
|---------------------|----------------------------------------------------------|------------|
|Generative AI (LLMs) |Content, knowledge codification, chat, document processing|High        |

## 3. Two-Phase Deployment Model

### Phase A — Immediate (Day 1)

Deploy capabilities that do **not** require historical data:

- **RPA:** Automate repetitive processes (invoicing, scheduling, data entry, PO generation)
- **Generative AI:** Templatize deliverables, codify knowledge, generate content, assist proposals/quotes, build internal Q&A systems
- **In parallel:** Instrument the business for data collection (implement CRM, digitize records, log transactions)

### Phase B — Data-Dependent (Month 6–12)

Once sufficient data has accumulated, layer in:

- **Supervised ML:** Lead scoring, churn prediction, demand forecasting, quality prediction
- **Statistical computing:** Pricing optimization, anomaly detection
- **Recommender systems:** Upsell/cross-sell logic
- **NLP analytics:** Sentiment analysis, document classification

**Key implication:** Every acquisition starts generating value immediately (Phase A). Phase B unlocks higher-impact levers as data accumulates. Targets with no existing data (R1b) are viable — they have a longer ramp but the same endpoint.

## 4. Reliability Conditions

Preconditions that predict whether AI deployment will deliver value. These become **sector screening criteria** in Phase 2.

### A. Data Conditions

|#  |Condition                      |Description                                                                                                                                            |
|---|-------------------------------|-------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|
|R1a|Data already exists            |Business holds usable digital data (transactions, customer records, operational logs). Faster path to Phase B.                                         |
|R1b|Data can be created            |Minimal digital trail today, but data can be generated by instrumenting operations post-acquisition. Adds 6–12 months before Phase B levers activate.  |
|R2 |Data volume matches application|ML/statistical models need sufficient observations. Low-volume businesses benefit from GenAI/RPA but not from prediction models until data accumulates.|

### B. Operational Conditions

|# |Condition                                    |Description                                                                                                                                                                                 |
|--|---------------------------------------------|--------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|
|R3|Processes are repetitive or pattern-based    |Higher share of revenue from repeatable processes = more AI levers available. Highly bespoke, one-off work is harder to augment.                                                            |
|R4|Significant human activity amenable to AI    |Humans perform tasks where AI can deliver gains in speed, cost-efficiency, reliability, or accuracy. Covers both labor-heavy (cost savings) and decision-heavy (better outcomes) businesses.|
|R5|Low AI adoption, baseline digitization exists|Sweet spot: basic digital tools in place (accounting, email, some CRM) but no AI. Businesses still on paper are deprioritized unless AI makes their digitization viable.                    |

### C. Market & Structural Conditions

|# |Condition                      |Description                                                                                                                                               |
|--|-------------------------------|----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|
|R6|Sector is fragmented           |Many similar sub-scale businesses. Competitors unlikely to adopt AI quickly. Enables repeatable playbook across portfolio — the strongest structural edge.|
|R7|Business is fundamentally sound|Adequate gross margins, repeat/recurring revenue, manageable customer concentration, viable market position. AI is a multiplier, not a rescue strategy.   |
|R8| Low AI disruption vulnerability | AI improves the business but does not threaten to commoditize or disintermediate its core value proposition. Sectors where AI could eventually replace the service itself (e.g., translation, basic accounting, call centres) are less attractive for long-term holds than sectors where the core work is physical, relational, or regulatory-gated.

### D. Due Diligence–Level Conditions (deal-specific)

Not sector screening criteria, but evaluated per target:

- **AI implementation cost:** Model tool/service costs and deployment resources. Fractional AI specialists shared across portfolio mitigate this.
- **Workforce adaptability:** Assess HR risk — staff willingness to adopt new tools, key-person dependencies. Appropriate incentive structures mitigate resistance.
- **Lever feasibility:** Which specific AI levers are realistically activatable for this target? GenAI/RPA are near-universal; ML depends on data.

## 5. Competitive Edge

|Factor                      |Description                                                                                                                                                                                                             |
|----------------------------|------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|
|**Timing**                  |Technology fluency enables deployment now, while most SME owners and traditional acquirers lack this capability. Early adopters capture value at lower entry prices.                                                    |
|**Execution capability**    |Knowing which tools to deploy, in what sequence, and how to manage change in small organizations. Operator skill, not technology.                                                                                       |
|**Repeatable playbook (R6)**|Acquiring multiple businesses in the same fragmented sector allows the playbook to be refined and reused. Each deployment gets faster, cheaper, and more effective. This portfolio-level capability compounds over time.|
